## Learning Objectives
- Understand the principles of effective prompt engineering for Physical AI
- Craft prompts that elicit accurate and useful responses from AI agents

- Iterate on prompts to improve AI agent performance

## Overview
Prompt engineering is a critical skill for effective human-AI collaboration in Physical AI development. Well-crafted prompts can significantly improve the quality and relevance of AI agent contributions to Physical AI projects.

## Theory
Effective prompt engineering for Physical AI involves several key principles:

**Context Provision**: Provide sufficient background information about the Physical AI domain, including relevant physics principles, constraints, and requirements. This helps AI agents understand the context of the task.

**Specificity**: Clearly define what you want the AI agent to do, including specific requirements, constraints, and expected outputs. Vague prompts often lead to irrelevant or generic responses.

**Structure and Format**: Use consistent formatting and structure in prompts to help AI agents understand what information is required and how it should be presented.

**Role Definition**: Specify the role the AI agent should take (e.g., expert physicist, experienced roboticist) to guide its response approach.

**Examples and Templates**: Include examples of desired outputs or templates to guide the AI agent's response format and content.

**Constraint Specification**: Clearly state any constraints, requirements, or limitations that the AI agent should consider in its response.

**Iterative Refinement**: Develop prompts iteratively, testing different formulations to see which produces the best results.

**Domain Knowledge Integration**: Incorporate relevant Physical AI concepts and terminology in prompts to guide the AI agent toward domain-appropriate responses.
